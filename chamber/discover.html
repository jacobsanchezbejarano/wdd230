Pupulation and location

Santa Cruz de la Sierra is a city located in the eastern plains of Bolivia, on the banks of the Piraí River. It is currently the most populous city in the country with a total of approximately 1.9 million inhabitants in 2021.2 It is the capital of the Department of Santa Cruz, the department with the highest Gross Regional Product in Bolivia, and one of the most developed cities in the country. , with a high municipal indicator of sustainable development, of 64.1.

History

Founded on February 26, 1561, by the Spanish captain Ñuflo de Chaves, the city underwent several location changes. In the 17th century, the area became the center of evangelizing missionary expeditions. On September 24, 1810, its first libertarian cry against Spanish rule. At the beginning of the 20th century, in the south, the war for the territory of Chaco broke out. Since the 1950s, due to physical integration with the rest of the country, the city experienced strong demographic growth. Currently, Santa Cruz de la Sierra is a dynamic, cosmopolitan city and is considered the economic and industrial engine of Bolivia since its metropolitan area concentrates a significant number of industries, including agriculture, forestry, mining, and hydrocarbons.

Santa Cruz has been registering a higher growth rate in the country

Since 2011, the GDP of Santa Cruz has been registering a higher growth rate the country, which made it possible to reduce the precipitous drop in the national GDP in 2020 so that it would not be greater than 8.8% (Santa Cruz decreased this year by only 4.3 %), says a report from the Bolivian Institute of Foreign Trade of Bolivia (IBCE). The projected population of the Department of Santa Cruz by 2022 is 3.4 million people, of which 56% is in the Municipality of Santa Cruz de la Sierra, with about 1.9 million inhabitants (in 1950 it had just 42,000 inhabitants).

Santa Cruz de la Sierra displaced La Paz to second place in its contribution to the national economy, and Cochabamba is in third place. The eastern region is responsible for providing the highest percentage of food to the national market but also exports a variety of agricultural products to neighboring markets. Information from the IBCE shows that in 2010 the GDP of Santa Cruz was around 5,000 million dollars and in 2019 it exceeded 10,000 million, and there are still no figures for 2021, but it is expected to exceed the figure of two years ago.
